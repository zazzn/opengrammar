Ollama — Which model for my GPU?

The extension reads your installed models live; use this to decide what to ollama pull. Recommended local model: qwen3.5:4b — with protected-text masking enabled it won our proofreading benchmark with perfect local score and no hard failures. At ~3.4 GB it runs comfortably on 8 GB cards like the GTX 1070 and most GPUs down to 6 GB. A bigger GPU does not need a bigger model — qwen3.5:4b beat qwen2.5:7b after masking. For the cloud, deepseek-chat (DeepSeek provider) is the top pick — the highest quality of everything we tested and only pennies per month.

VRAM headroom: leave ~1–2 GB free. A model that doesn't fit spills to system RAM and gets very slow. Skip coder/embed/vision variants — the popup hides them from the writing-model picker because they are not tuned for prose correction.

Gaming / GPU-heavy tasks: Ollama keeps the model in VRAM for the idle period after each use. The extension can't detect a game launching (it's a browser extension), so to free VRAM: set “Unload model when idle” (in the popup) to Immediately or 30 s, and/or click “Unload now” in the popup before you start a game. This is exactly why running Ollama on a separate machine avoids the contention entirely.

Debug & Tuning

Capture what OGrammar produces (inline fixes, corrections, tone rewrites, autocomplete) so you can share a compact log for tuning. Off by default; no overhead when off. Nothing is sent anywhere — the log stays on this device until you copy it.
